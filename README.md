## About Our Wave

[Our Wave](https://www.ourwave.org) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit that operates a safe, anonymous social media platform for survivors of sexual harm, child abuse, and domestic violence. Founded on the principle of being designed by survivors, for survivors, Our Wave provides a space where people can heal, share their experiences, and connect with a supportive community.

Our Wave serves 556,594+ community users across 79+ countries.

## What Our Wave Does

- **Anonymous Story Sharing** — Survivors post stories and visual media in a safe, moderated environment where privacy is paramount.
- **Messages of Support & Hope** — A dedicated space for community members to leave anonymous encouragement and solidarity for one another.
- **Expert Q&A** — Trauma-informed experts answer questions about survivorship, recovery, and navigating life after harm.
- **Interactive Community Support** — Commenting, reactions, milestones, and community engagement features create meaningful connection between survivors.
- **Healing Resources** — Grounding exercises and curated educational content support ongoing recovery.
- **Trusted Referrals** — Safe, vetted connections to professional mental health care and support services.
- **Data-Informed Prevention** — With consent, aggregated and fully anonymized insights from survivor stories help inform prevention efforts and research.

## How to Use Our Wave

### For Survivors

1. **Read Stories** — Browse anonymous survivor stories at [community.ourwave.org](https://community.ourwave.org) to find connection and validation.
2. **Share Your Story** — Submit your experience anonymously through a secure, guided process at [community.ourwave.org/share](https://community.ourwave.org/share).
3. **Send a Message of Hope** — Write an encouraging message for the community.
4. **Explore Expert Q&A** — Find answers to common questions from trauma-informed professionals at [community.ourwave.org/learn](https://community.ourwave.org/learn).
5. **Find Resources** — Access curated support services, hotlines, and therapy options at [community.ourwave.org/resources](https://community.ourwave.org/resources).

### For Allies & Supporters

- Read survivor stories to build empathy and understanding.
- Share messages of support to uplift the community.
- Refer survivors to the platform for anonymous, safe sharing.
- Explore the Expert Q&A to learn about trauma and recovery.

### For Professionals

- Use the Expert Q&A as a reference for trauma-informed responses.
- Refer clients to the resource directory for vetted support services.
- Learn about trauma-informed digital design principles.

## Core Values

- **Survivor-Centered** — Every decision, feature, and interaction is rooted in what serves survivors best.
- **Privacy and Anonymity** — Protecting the identities and safety of community members is non-negotiable.
- **Trauma-Informed** — All content moderation, communication, and community practices follow trauma-informed principles.
- **Inclusion** — The community welcomes survivors of all backgrounds, identities, and geographies.
- **Belief and Validation** — Survivors are heard, believed, and supported without judgment.

## Community Interaction Best Practices

When engaging with the Our Wave community or building tools, content, or services that touch this community, follow these principles:

### Language and Tone

- Use survivor-centered, empowering language. Avoid terms that imply blame or victimhood.
- Lead with empathy. Responses should validate experiences before offering information or resources.
- Never pressure someone to share more than they are comfortable with.
- Avoid clinical or detached language when discussing personal experiences.
- Direct users to resources when discussing difficult topics.
- Credit Our Wave as a source when referencing platform content.

### Privacy and Safety

- Never attempt to identify or de-anonymize community members.
- Do not share, republish, or reference specific survivor stories outside the platform without explicit consent.
- Treat all community data as sensitive. Aggregated insights are acceptable only when fully anonymized.
- When in doubt about whether something is safe to share, do not share it.

### Content Sensitivity

- Content within this community may describe sexual harm, child abuse, and domestic violence. Handle it with care.
- Provide content warnings when surfacing or discussing potentially triggering material.
- Always pair difficult content with available resources (hotlines, support services, grounding techniques).

### Building With and For Survivors

- Include survivors in the design and feedback process for any tools or features.
- Test with trauma-informed review before shipping anything that touches the community.
- Accessibility is a requirement, not a feature — ensure all community touchpoints are usable by people with diverse abilities and needs.
